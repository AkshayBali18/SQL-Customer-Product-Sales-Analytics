# 📊 Sales Analytics SQL Project

## 📖 Overview
This project focuses on analyzing sales transactions using SQL.  
The goal is to derive **actionable business insights** such as product performance, customer base growth, and revenue trends.  

The key deliverable is a SQL **view** called `gold_report_products`,`gold_report_customers` which aggregates important KPIs like:
- Total sales
- Customer count
- Product lifespan
- Recency of last order
- Product segmentation (High Performer, Mid Range, Low Performer)
- Average order revenue
- Average monthly revenue

---

## 📂 Dataset
The project uses a **star schema** design with the following tables:

- **`gold_fact_sales`** – transactional sales data  
- **`gold_dim_products`** – product catalog with categories and costs  
- **`gold_dim_customers`** – customer demographic details  

*(If you don’t want to share real datasets, add sample/synthetic data in a `data/` folder for reproducibility.)*

---

## ⚡ SQL Features Used
- Joins: `RIGHT JOIN` to combine fact & dimension data  
- Aggregations: `SUM`, `COUNT(DISTINCT)`, `AVG`, `MAX`, `MIN`  
- Date functions: `TIMESTAMPDIFF`, `CURDATE`  
- Conditional logic: `CASE WHEN` for segmentation  
- Derived KPIs: Recency, Avg Order Revenue, Avg Monthly Revenue  

---

## 📊 Product Report Outputs
The main output is the **SQL view** `gold_report_products`.

Example KPIs produced:
- **High Performer Products**: total sales > 50,000  
- **Mid Range Products**: total sales between 10,000 – 50,000  
- **Low Performer Products**: total sales < 10,000  
- Recency of last order in months  
- Average selling price per unit  
- Average monthly revenue across product lifespan

## 📊 Customer Report Outputs

The primary output of this script is the **SQL view** `gold.report_customers`. This view consolidates customer demographics, purchase history, and calculated performance metrics.

### 1. Customer Segmentation
Customers are categorized based on their tenure (lifespan) and spending habits:
* **VIP**: Customers with a lifespan $\ge$ 12 months and total sales $\ge$ 5,000.
* **Regular**: Customers with a lifespan $\ge$ 12 months and total sales < 5,000.
* **New**: Customers with a lifespan of less than 12 months.

### 2. Demographic Grouping
The report segments customers into the following age brackets based on their birth date:
* **Below 20**
* **20-29**
* **30-39**
* **40-49**
* **Above 50**

### 3. Key KPIs Produced
The view calculates several essential metrics to evaluate customer value:
* **Total Sales**: The sum of all revenue generated by the customer.
* **Total Orders**: The count of distinct order numbers per customer.
* **Recency**: The number of months since the customer's last order date relative to the current date.
* **Lifespan**: The duration in months between the customer's first and last order.
* **Average Order Value (AOV)**: Calculated as $\text{Total Sales} / \text{Total Orders}$.
* **Average Monthly Spend**: Calculated as $\text{Total Sales} / \text{Lifespan}$.
